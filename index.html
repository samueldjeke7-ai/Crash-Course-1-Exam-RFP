A note on field knowledge. The map is a starting point for conversation, not an answer. Where it disagrees with what EBI field teams know on the ground — a department with more functioning schools than the Enveritas dataset captures, or a displacement route the data doesn't show — the disagreement itself is the most useful finding. It points either to a data gap or to a ground condition the data cannot see. Both improve the method.

How to read this map

Each department is scored on two indicators: the ratio of school-age children to schools mapped by Enveritas, and the department's latitude, normalised nationally, as a proxy for proximity to the Mali / Burkina Faso border. The two indicators are combined into one number; the top third of scored departments are red, the middle third orange, the bottom third yellow.

Method: unweighted mean of two min-max normalised indicators per department, tercile classified across 107 of 108 departments. Abidjan is excluded because the Enveritas dataset does not reliably capture dense urban school infrastructure (717 children per mapped school there, vs. 23–500 in every other department). ACLED has no open Côte d'Ivoire layer, so cross-border refugee counts (UNHCR) stand in for a direct conflict overlay. See docs/data_decisions.md for the full reasoning behind both decisions.
