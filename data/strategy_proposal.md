# Strategy Proposal: Bridging the Education Gap in Northern Côte d'Ivoire

## 1. The Challenge
Northern Côte d'Ivoire faces a critical education crisis. While the national average suggests progress, the northern regions (Bouaké, Korhogo, Man, Danané) suffer from an enrollment rate **below 40%**, which is **2x lower** than the south. Over **700,000 children** were displaced during peak conflict years, and infrastructure recovery has been uneven.

## 2. Our Data Strategy
To address this, we propose a three-pillar data approach:
1.  **Gap Mapping**: Using WorldPop population data against Enveritas school locations to calculate the "Children per School" ratio.
2.  **Conflict Overlay**: Integrating ACLED real-time conflict data to identify schools at high risk of closure or teacher abandonment.
3.  **Dynamic Prioritization**: An AI-assisted scoring system that flags departments where the gap between child population and school capacity is widest.

## 3. Visual Choice Rationale
For the interactive map (Leaflet), we chose:
*   **Simple Markers**: To represent individual schools, allowing field staff to identify specific sites.
*   **Heat Zones (Red)**: To highlight Northern regions where the enrollment gap is most severe, creating an immediate visual "call to action."
*   **Interactive Popups**: To provide the name and status of schools without cluttering the map.
*   **OpenStreetMap Base**: To ensure familiarity with local geography and roads for logistics planning.

## 4. Expected Impact
By providing a "live" picture of education access, the Education Bridge Initiative (EBI) can move from reactive relief to proactive infrastructure development, ensuring that resources reach the most under-served children first.
